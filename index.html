Biography

I am a PhD student in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Purdue University. I work as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Herrick Laboratories under Prof. Fabio Semperlotti . My primary research interest is in the field of Non-linear Mechanics. Most of my work so far has been on Nonlinear Computational Mechanics. Currently, I am working on Fractional Calculus and its applications in Mechanical Engineering.

I have graduated as a Bachelor in Mechanical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur in 2017. I am fortunate to have worked under Prof. Nalinaksh S. Vyas and Prof. Sumit Basu. I have taken courses on Continuum and Fracture Mechanics from Prof. P. Venkitanarayanan and courses on Dynamics and Nonlinear Vibrations from Prof. Anindya Chatterjee. They have always inspired me. Post Bachelors, I have worked for an year with ITC Ltd in their Personal Care and Products Business. I have looked after Automation and CAPEX. I am an Indian Railways fan and love the mayhem of these metal horses. I have worked on simulation of Indian Broad Gauge Bogie Wheel-sets. I have also served as the Vice-Captain of BAJA SAE, IITK Motorsports. Human Psychology is my area of interest as well. I follow Indian Politics religiously and take interest in psephology.
